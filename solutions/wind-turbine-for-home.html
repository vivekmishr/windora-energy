Why a Wind Turbine for Your Home Makes Sense

Indian residential electricity tariffs have risen 30-50% over the last five years. Most homes now spend ₹3,000-₹15,000+ per month on electricity, with daily power cuts making the situation worse. A residential wind turbine cuts your dependence on the grid — generating clean power day and night, even during monsoon when solar output drops.

Windora's vertical axis wind turbines are built specifically for Indian homes. They start generating at wind speeds as low as 3.5 m/s, operate near-silently under 35 dB, and fit on rooftops, terraces, and small backyards.

Typical residential setup: A 3 kW Windora helical turbine + optional 2 kW solar panels + battery storage. Generates 5,000–7,000 kWh per year. Cuts a ₹6,000/month bill down to ₹1,500–2,000. Pays back in 4–6 years.

Best Wind Turbine for Indian Homes

For 1-2 BHK apartments / small homes

Helical 1.5 kW vertical turbine — fits on apartment terraces with society approval. Annual output ~2,500-3,200 kWh. Suitable for monthly bills of ₹2,500-4,000.

For 3-4 BHK homes / villas

Helical 3 kW or Tulip 2 kW — most popular residential size in India. Annual output ~4,500-6,500 kWh. Cuts ₹5,000-8,000 monthly bills by 60-80%.

For bungalows / large villas / farmhouses

Helical 5 kW or Tulip 5 kW — handles heavy load homes with ACs, EV charging, and large appliances. Annual output ~9,000-12,000 kWh.

Maximum benefit — go hybrid

For homes wanting near-zero grid dependence, combine solar + wind in a hybrid system. Solar handles daytime, wind handles evenings, nights and monsoon. Pays back faster than either alone.

Requirements for Installing at Home

  • Open space — rooftop area of 15-25 sq metres, or backyard / lawn space of 10-15 sq metres
  • Wind exposure — minimal obstruction from trees and buildings in primary wind direction
  • Structural strength — for rooftop installs, structure should support 200-400 kg point load
  • Society / building permission — for apartments and societies (we help with paperwork)
  • Electrical connection — existing single-phase or three-phase grid connection

The Installation Process — End to End

  1. Free site visit — our engineer measures wind, assesses location, photographs the site
  2. Custom design — turbine model, tower height, and mounting plan tailored to your home
  3. Written quote — fully transparent, including all materials and installation
  4. Civil work — foundation, mast, electrical conduit (1-2 days)
  5. Turbine installation — assembly, mounting, commissioning (1 day)
  6. Grid sync & testing — net-metering setup, output verification
  7. Handover — operator training, warranty card, monitoring app login

FAQ: Wind Turbine for Home in India

What is the best wind turbine for home use in India?

For most Indian homes, a Windora Helical 3 kW VAWT is the sweet spot — affordable, low maintenance, fits on rooftops, runs near-silently. For larger homes or those wanting 24×7 generation, a 5 kW Hybrid Solar+Wind system is better.

Does my home have enough wind?

Likely yes. Windora turbines start at just 3.5 m/s wind. Most Indian cities see annual average winds of 3-5 m/s, which puts them well into productive range. Free site survey confirms.

Can I install a wind turbine on an apartment terrace?

Yes, with society/board permission. The 1.5 kW Helical model is specifically engineered for apartment rooftops — low vibration mounts, compact footprint, near-silent.

Will wind turbines damage my roof?

No — our installations use vibration-isolated mounts and distribute load across structural members. We always assess roof strength before recommending rooftop install.
