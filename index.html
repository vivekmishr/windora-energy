FAQ

Common Questions About Wind & Solar Energy in India

Everything you need to know about vertical wind turbines, solar panels, and hybrid green energy systems for your home, farm, or business

How much electricity can a vertical axis wind turbine generate in India?

A 3 kW Windora vertical axis wind turbine typically generates 4,500–7,000 kWh per year in most Indian locations with average winds of 3–5 m/s. Exact output depends on site wind conditions, tower height, and turbine model. Sites in coastal areas (Mumbai, Chennai, Visakhapatnam), on high plateaus (Pune, Bangalore, Hyderabad), and in windy plains (Jaipur, Ahmedabad, Rajkot) typically see higher generation. We provide a free site analysis to give you an accurate estimate for your location.

What wind speed is needed for a Windora vertical wind turbine to work?

Windora vertical axis wind turbines start generating at wind speeds as low as 1.5 m/s — ideal for Indian conditions where average winds are often 3–6 m/s. Traditional horizontal turbines require 3–4 m/s just to start, making them unsuitable for most Indian homes and farms. Our helical and tulip designs are specifically engineered for India's low and variable wind conditions.

Can I install a wind turbine on my home in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore or any Indian city?

Yes. Our vertical axis wind turbines are designed specifically for Indian urban rooftops. They are compact, whisper-silent (under 35 dB — quieter than a normal conversation), and work efficiently in turbulent city winds. We install across Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Pune, Chennai, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Kolkata, Jaipur, Surat, Lucknow, Kanpur, Nagpur, Indore, Thane, Bhopal, Visakhapatnam, Patna, Vadodara, Ghaziabad, Ludhiana, Agra, Nashik, Faridabad, Meerut, Rajkot, Coimbatore, Chandigarh, Guwahati, Noida, Gurgaon and all other major Indian cities.

What is the ROI or payback period for a hybrid solar-wind system in India?

Typical ROI for a residential Windora hybrid solar + wind system in India is 4–6 years. Commercial installations achieve 4–7 year payback, and farms or agricultural installations often achieve 3–5 years due to higher daily consumption. After the payback period, you enjoy 20+ years of near-free electricity. With rising grid tariffs across Indian states, payback periods continue to shorten.

Are vertical axis wind turbines better than horizontal-axis turbines for homes and commercial buildings?

For homes, farms, and city buildings — yes, by far. Vertical axis wind turbines (VAWTs) capture wind from any direction without needing to rotate, operate near-silently, are bird and bat safe, work in turbulent urban winds, and fit compactly on rooftops and tight spaces. Horizontal turbines (the classic three-blade design) need wide open spaces, constant wind direction, and are noisy — making them impractical for most Indian residential and commercial sites.

Is wind energy better than solar energy in India? Should I choose one or both?

Both have unique strengths — and a hybrid system gives the best of both:

  • Solar panels work during daytime with peak output in summer
  • Wind turbines work 24×7, strongest at night and during monsoon
  • Hybrid systems combine solar panels and vertical wind turbines for reliable power year-round

For most Indian homes and businesses, we recommend a hybrid solar-wind system for consistent generation through all seasons — including monsoons and winter when solar alone underperforms.

What maintenance does a Windora vertical wind turbine require?

Very low maintenance. Annual visual inspection and lubrication is typically all that's needed. Our turbines are designed for a 25-year operational life with minimal intervention. We offer annual maintenance contracts across all Indian cities, and our generators and gearboxes are accessible from ground level — unlike tall horizontal turbines that need cranes for servicing.

Which cities and states in India get enough wind for Windora turbines to be viable?

Almost all of India. Because Windora turbines start generating at just 1.5 m/s, they work in nearly every Indian city — coastal (Mumbai, Chennai, Visakhapatnam, Kochi, Goa, Mangalore), plateau and hills (Pune, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mysore, Shimla), plains (Delhi, Jaipur, Lucknow, Kanpur, Ahmedabad, Indore), and desert zones (Jodhpur, Jaisalmer, Bikaner). Maharashtra, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Rajasthan, and Andhra Pradesh have especially strong wind resources. We verify feasibility with a free site analysis.

Do you install solar panels in addition to wind turbines?

Yes. We offer complete green energy solutions: standalone rooftop solar panel systems, standalone vertical wind turbines, and integrated hybrid solar-wind systems with battery backup. We also design off-grid systems for remote sites, farms, and locations without reliable grid access. Every system is custom-engineered for your site and energy needs.

Can a wind turbine power my entire house or business?

Yes, depending on sizing. A typical Indian home consuming 300–500 units/month can be powered by a 3–5 kW Windora system. Businesses and farms with larger loads use our 10–50 kW commercial turbines or multi-turbine arrays. For complete off-grid operation, we add battery storage sized to your daily consumption and required backup hours.
