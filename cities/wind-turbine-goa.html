Why Wind Energy Works in Goa

Goa sits on the Konkan coast, where a reliable Arabian Sea sea-breeze blows onshore for much of the year and intensifies dramatically through the southwest monsoon. Annual averages are around 3-5 m/s along the coast (higher in the monsoon months), which makes Goa a good fit for Windora's vertical axis wind turbines, which start generating at just 3.5 m/s — especially as part of a hybrid solar+wind system.

  • Coastal sea-breeze plus strong monsoon winds (June–September) exactly when solar output drops
  • Heavy tourism load — resorts and hotels run high air-conditioning demand year-round
  • Frequent monsoon-season outages make on-site generation and battery backup valuable
  • Goa's clean-and-green tourism positioning rewards visible renewable energy
  • Near-silent, cyclone-rated turbines suited to coastal villas, homes, and farms

Why it pays off: A 5 kW hybrid solar+wind system in Goa can generate roughly 8,000–12,000 kWh a year — solar carries the sunny dry season while the turbine keeps producing through the cloudy, windy monsoon, smoothing output and cutting reliance on the grid during the season of most outages.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Goa

Is Goa windy enough for a small wind turbine?

Goa's Arabian Sea coastline gives it a steady onshore sea-breeze that strengthens sharply through the long southwest monsoon (June–September). Our vertical axis turbines start generating at just 3.5 m/s and run in a hybrid solar+wind setup, so they perform reliably across North and South Goa year-round.

Which DISCOM handles net-metering in Goa?

The Goa Electricity Department manages the local grid connection. We handle the entire net-metering application and paperwork as part of our turnkey installation.

Should I choose wind, solar, or hybrid in Goa?

A hybrid solar+wind system is ideal for Goa. Solar carries the dry sunny months, while wind takes over through the long monsoon when solar output drops 60–70% and cloud cover is heaviest — giving the most consistent year-round power for homes and resorts.

Can a wind turbine power a Goa resort or villa?

Yes. Coastal resorts and villas with high air-conditioning loads and open sea-facing exposure are excellent candidates. We design 5–100 kW hybrid solar+wind arrays for hotels, resorts, and large homes across the Goa coast.
