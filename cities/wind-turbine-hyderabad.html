Why Wind Energy Works in Hyderabad

Hyderabad sits at ~540 metres on the Deccan plateau, with annual average winds of 3-5 m/s. The city's summer pre-monsoon winds and consistent dry-season breezes make it suitable for our vertical axis turbines, which start at just 1.5 m/s.

  • TSSPDCL tariffs rising; commercial slabs reach ₹9-10/unit
  • Hot summers = high AC load = strong incentive to self-generate
  • IT/pharma campuses increasingly setting ESG renewable targets
  • Farmhouse belt — Shankarpalli, Moinabad, Shamirpet, Chevella zones
  • Industrial zones — Patancheru, Pashamylaram, Bollaram, Genome Valley

Real example: A 5 kW Hybrid Solar+Wind on a Banjara Hills villa generates ~11,500 kWh/year — covering most household load. Payback in 5-6 years on Telangana tariffs.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Hyderabad

Will the turbine handle Hyderabad's summer heat?

Yes — our turbines are rated for ambient up to 55°C. Generators have thermal protection and de-rate gracefully if needed. We've installed across Telangana and AP heat zones for years.

Is the wind enough in Hyderabad?

Yes — 3-5 m/s is well above our 1.5 m/s start-up. Pre-monsoon (March-May) winds are especially strong, exactly when AC load peaks. Hybrid systems with solar are most popular here.

Can my Shankarpalli farmhouse go fully off-grid?

Many do — see our off-grid systems. Combine 5-10 kW wind + 10 kW solar + 30 kWh battery, and your farmhouse becomes grid-independent.
