Why Wind Energy Works in Sikar

Sikar, in the Shekhawati region, has dry, steady winds well-suited to wind and hybrid systems. With annual averages around 4-6 m/s, Sikar is a very good fit for Windora's vertical axis wind turbines, which start generating at just 3.5 m/s.

  • Very good wind resource — Shekhawati is among India's stronger wind zones
  • JVVNL grid tariffs climb steeply at higher consumption slabs
  • High summer cooling loads across Rajasthan — fast payback on self-generation
  • Rajasthan renewable-energy incentives + accelerated depreciation for businesses
  • Strong fit for farms, farmhouses, homes, and industry around Sikar

Why it pays off: A 5 kW hybrid solar+wind system in Sikar can generate roughly 10,000–16,000 kWh a year given the local 4-6 m/s wind resource — typically cutting a high JVVNL bill by 70–90% with a 4–6 year payback.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Sikar

Is Sikar windy enough for a small wind turbine?

Sikar, in the Shekhawati region, has dry, steady winds well-suited to wind and hybrid systems. Our vertical axis turbines start generating at just 3.5 m/s, so they perform reliably across Sikar and Sikar district.

Which DISCOM handles net-metering in Sikar?

JVVNL manages the local grid connection in Sikar. We handle the entire net-metering application and paperwork as part of our turnkey installation.

Should I choose wind, solar, or hybrid in Sikar?

For most Rajasthan sites we recommend a hybrid solar+wind system — solar covers sunny afternoons while wind covers evenings, nights, and the monsoon, giving the most consistent year-round output.

Can you install off-grid for a remote Sikar farm?

Yes. Given the strong 4-6 m/s wind resource around Sikar, off-grid and battery-backed hybrid systems work very well for remote farms and farmhouses with weak or no grid supply.
