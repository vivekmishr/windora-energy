Chennai's Wind Profile — Among the Best in Urban India

Chennai sits on India's east coast where the strong Coromandel sea breeze pushes onshore almost daily. Annual average wind speeds in Chennai reach 5-8 m/s — comparable to dedicated wind-farm sites in Tamil Nadu's interior. This makes Chennai exceptional for small wind energy.

  • Cyclone exposure — our turbines rated to 55 m/s survival winds (cyclone-grade)
  • High solar humidity reduces solar output; wind compensates beautifully
  • TANGEDCO tariffs rising, making payback under 5 years common
  • Coastal homes & ECR villas — exceptional wind exposure on East Coast Road
  • Heavy industry corridor — Sriperumbudur, Oragadam, Maraimalai Nagar

Real example: A 5 kW Tulip turbine on an ECR beach villa generates ~14,000-17,000 kWh/year. Cuts a ₹12,000/month TANGEDCO bill to ₹2,000. Payback in 4-5 years.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Chennai

Will it survive a Chennai cyclone?

Yes. All Chennai installations are cyclone-rated (55 m/s = ~198 km/h survival). Automatic over-speed protection brakes the rotor when winds exceed safe operating range.

How does salt air affect the turbine?

Our coastal installations use marine-grade coatings and stainless hardware. Installations within 200m of the sea have been operating 8+ years with no corrosion.

Can I get TANGEDCO net metering?

Yes — Tamil Nadu has an active net-metering policy. We handle the TANGEDCO application end-to-end.
