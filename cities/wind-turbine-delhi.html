Why Wind Energy Works in Delhi NCR

Delhi sits on the open Gangetic plains with annual average winds of 3-5 m/s — perfectly within Windora's low-wind turbine operating range. The region's pre-monsoon and monsoon months bring strong winds, while the brutal summer power demand makes self-generation economically critical.

  • BSES / TPDDL / NPCL tariffs reach ₹8-12+/unit at high consumption — fast payback
  • Pollution concerns drive eco-installations across Delhi NCR
  • Frequent power cuts — especially Gurgaon, Faridabad, Noida outer sectors
  • Farmhouse belt — Mehrauli, Chhatarpur, Bijwasan, Gurgaon farmhouses ideal for self-power

Real example: A 5 kW hybrid solar+wind on a Chhatarpur farmhouse generates ~14,000 kWh/year. Cuts a ₹15,000/month bill to ₹3,000. Payback in 5 years.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Delhi NCR

Is Delhi air pollution a problem for turbines?

No — turbines aren't sensitive to dust the way solar panels are. Our blades have self-cleaning aerodynamics and just need a wash 1-2x per year. Pollution actually reduces solar output but barely affects wind.

Will it work in Delhi winter when wind drops?

Yes — Delhi winters still see 2-3 m/s minimum winds, above our 3.5 m/s start-up. Plus winter is when grid load peaks (heaters, geysers), so even reduced generation makes a big bill impact.

Can I get net metering with BSES/TPDDL?

Yes. Delhi has an active rooftop renewable net-metering policy. We handle the BSES/TPDDL application paperwork end-to-end.
