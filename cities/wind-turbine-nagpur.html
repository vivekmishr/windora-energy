Why Wind Energy Works in Nagpur

Nagpur is the geographic centre of India, on the eastern Deccan plateau with open landscapes that favour steady wind flow. Annual averages range from 3-5 m/s, with strong pre-monsoon and monsoon winds. That makes Nagpur a good fit for Windora's vertical axis wind turbines, which start generating at just 3.5 m/s.

  • MSEDCL tariffs reach ₹13+/unit at high slabs
  • Hot summers (45°C+) = extreme AC load = high bills
  • MIHAN SEZ and Butibori industrial corridor growth
  • Open land plots in Wardha Road, Hingna, Khapri farmhouses
  • Maharashtra renewable energy state policy support

Real example: A 5 kW Tulip on a Civil Lines bungalow generates ~10,500 kWh/year. Cuts an ₹8,000/month bill to ~₹1,500. Payback 5 years.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Nagpur

Will it handle Nagpur summer heat?

Yes — our turbines are rated for ambient up to 55°C. Summers are when generation matters most (high AC load) and our systems are designed for it.

Is wind better than solar for Nagpur?

Hybrid is best. Solar handles peak summer afternoons; wind handles evenings and monsoon. Nagpur's diverse load profile benefits from both.

Can I install at my MIHAN factory?

Yes — MIHAN's open layout is ideal for commercial-scale arrays. We've designed 25-100 kW installations for the SEZ.
