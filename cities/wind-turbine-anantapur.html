Why Wind Energy Works in Anantapur

Anantapur is one of India's windiest districts — the Rayalaseema plateau channels strong, steady winds and already hosts large wind parks such as Beluguppa, making it outstanding for small wind and hybrid systems. With annual averages around 6-8 m/s, Anantapur is a excellent fit for Windora's vertical axis wind turbines, which start generating at just 3.5 m/s.

  • Excellent wind resource — Rayalaseema wind corridor is among India's stronger wind zones
  • APSPDCL grid tariffs climb steeply at higher consumption slabs
  • High summer cooling loads across Andhra Pradesh — fast payback on self-generation
  • Andhra Pradesh renewable-energy incentives + accelerated depreciation for businesses
  • Strong fit for farms, farmhouses, homes, and industry around Anantapur

Why it pays off: A 5 kW hybrid solar+wind system in Anantapur can generate roughly 10,000–16,000 kWh a year given the local 6-8 m/s wind resource — typically cutting a high APSPDCL bill by 70–90% with a 4–6 year payback.

FAQ — Wind Turbine Anantapur

Is Anantapur windy enough for a small wind turbine?

Anantapur is one of India's windiest districts — the Rayalaseema plateau channels strong, steady winds and already hosts large wind parks such as Beluguppa, making it outstanding for small wind and hybrid systems. Our vertical axis turbines start generating at just 3.5 m/s, so they perform reliably across Anantapur and Anantapur district.

Which DISCOM handles net-metering in Anantapur?

APSPDCL manages the local grid connection in Anantapur. We handle the entire net-metering application and paperwork as part of our turnkey installation.

Should I choose wind, solar, or hybrid in Anantapur?

For most Andhra Pradesh sites we recommend a hybrid solar+wind system — solar covers sunny afternoons while wind covers evenings, nights, and the monsoon, giving the most consistent year-round output.

Can you install off-grid for a remote Anantapur farm?

Yes. Given the strong 6-8 m/s wind resource around Anantapur, off-grid and battery-backed hybrid systems work very well for remote farms and farmhouses with weak or no grid supply.
